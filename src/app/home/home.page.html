Fitts' Law Test

 Welcome to the research study! Non-disclosure Agreement

Good Morning/Afternoon.
We are conducting a research study for our IT482 class to determine the accuracy of Fitts' Law. Fitts' Law is a theoretical formula that estimates the time it will take a user to move their cursor and click on an Icon on screen based on the size and distance of the icon from the initial cursor position.

Prior to the administration of the Study we will be polling users for demographic information. Please be assured that your responses will be kept completely confidential, this information is only going to be used to determine if there is any significant differences in performance between various groups of individuals (i.e.-women are faster than men).

Participation in this study is completely voluntary, and your decision whether or not to participate will not affect your relationship with Minnesota State University, Mankato, and refusal to participate will involve no penalty or loss of benefits.

Your time is very important to us, so to prepare you we plan on the survey taking approximately 1 minute to complete, a 1 minute tutorital, and approximately 25 minutes for the study itself. This means we expect it to take between 27-30minutes of your time to complete. Again your participation in this research is voluntary. You have the right to withdraw at any point during the study, for any reason, and without any prejudice.

CAUTION: This test will require participants to move a mouse cursor back and forth across the screen and click on certain icons. As this will require repeatative motion those who have perviously existing conditions such as carpal tunnel may find this study aggrivates those conditions. Please let us know at any point during the study if you experience pain in the hand or wrist and cannot continue.
